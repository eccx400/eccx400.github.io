About Me

Who am I?

My name is Eric Cheng, and I am a recent graduate in Computer Science from Santa Clara University in California, USA. I was born in Taipei, Taiwan, but spent most of my life in the United States with a brief interlude in Beijing, China. In the past few years I have had experience with various programming projects and languages (C/C++, Java, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS,etc). I have also emmersed myself in modules such as Pandas xlsxwriter for parsing and analyzing python files, Qt Quick for developing a working user interface, and TCP in Ubuntu for remote transfer of log files.

Why Computer Science?

My passions are rooted in technological innovation, social responsibility, and collaboration. My past experience included being an intern at the VA Palo Alto hospital, developing data analytic algorithms to scrape patient feedback from the hospital provided opinion forms and social media opened my eyes to the profound impact of natural language processing in healthcare analytics. Recently, I have been working to develop a Python application to automate ADDS (Adult Deterioration Detection System) chart modeling and trend analysis on incoming patient data by performing machine learning algorithms on patientsâ€™ four vitals during their stay in the ICU. My goal is to develop a user dashboard which automatically generates graphical displays and interprets abnormal patient observations, effectively allowing medical professionals to monitor and visualize patient deterioration without the unnecessary cognitive effort needed to interpret traditional observation charts.

Other facts about me

Having spent a portion of my life abroad, I was exposed to cultural influences that have shaped me into who I am today. I am an avid traveler, always abiding by my motto "traveling should be fun, not stressful". My preference of solo tripping allows me to do that, I like exploring places off the beaten track, and seeing things other tourists simply don't see.

In my spare time, I enjoy practicing my hobbies and conducting physical exercises. I am a huge fan of Go (also known as Weiqi), which is an ancient board game that while seemingly simple to play, is one of the most complex strategic games to ever be developed. Go challenges a player's intuition, memory, problem-solving, and decision making skills. For most of my childhood, Go was an important part of my life, and I even earned a rank of 1-dan through competitions organized by the Chinese Go Association during my life in China. I have also been following AI developments in Go technology, especially the reknowned DeepMind AlphaGo. To those who are willing to give it a try, you can play the game online here. Have fun!
